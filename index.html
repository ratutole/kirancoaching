About Me

Hi, I’m Bandisha — Somatic Attachment & Relationship Coach

I founded Kiran Coaching in honor of my father. The word Kiran means “ray of light,” capturing the essence of healing: gently removing layers of pain so you can step fully into your own light.

While my name is Bandisha, my practice is called Kiran Coaching — a reminder that the light within us shines even when hidden.

I combine somatic attachment healing, nervous system regulation, and subconscious reprogramming to support you in feeling safe, connected, and whole.

Rooted in psychology and social behavior, guided by compassion, curiosity, and respect for the complexity of human experience.

WHY YOU’RE HERE

You're Not Broken. You're Adapting.

Most relationship struggles aren’t because you’re failing—they’re because your nervous system and subconscious mind adapted to protect you.

Repeating painful patterns in love or family

Feeling anxious, overwhelmed, or shut down in intimacy

Attracting emotionally unavailable partners

Struggling with self-worth, boundaries, or trust

Craving closeness but feeling unsafe with it

These are not flaws. They are survival strategies.

I combine relationship coaching with somatic attachment healing and subconscious reprogramming to help you feel safe in your body, confident in your truth, and secure in your connections.

REPROGRAM YOUR MIND. REWIRE YOUR BODY. REWRITE YOUR STORY.

The beliefs you hold—about your worth, your loveability, your safety—were shaped long ago, before you had language.

Common core beliefs might include:

  • “I have to be perfect to be loved.”
  • “If I express myself, I’ll be rejected.”
  • “I’m too much.”
  • “I’m not enough.”

These beliefs don’t just live in your thoughts — they live in your nervous system: your breath, posture, impulses to shrink or control.

In this work, we gently release these beliefs and you’ll experience:

  • Real self-worth — rooted in presence, not performance
  • Secure confidence — grounded and embodied, not performative
  • True self-esteem — built from inner safety, not outer validation
  • New stories — about who you are and what love can feel like

This isn’t about fixing you. It’s about remembering who you were before the world taught you to forget.
